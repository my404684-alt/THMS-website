Entry No. 842

Great Pyramid of Giza

History Maps Sketches
October 19th, 2026

Preserving the
Eternal Monoliths

Commissioned during the Fourth Dynasty for the Pharaoh Khufu, this limestone giant remained the tallest man-made structure on Earth for over 3,800 years. Its construction involved the precise placement of 2.3 million stone blocks, each weighing an average of 2.5 tons—a feat of engineering that defies the limitations of its era.

While time has stripped the pyramid of its polished white Tura limestone casing, the core remains a testament to celestial alignment, its four faces oriented almost exactly to true north. In our modern attempts to digitize this heritage, we find that the handwriting of the original architects is more permanent than any server we could build.

Visual Appendices

Current efforts focus on the ScanPyramids Project. Using cosmic-ray muon radiography, researchers have discovered a previously unknown internal void situated above the Grand Gallery. This "Big Void" is at least 30 meters long, suggesting that the pyramid still guards secrets that neither grave robbers nor early archaeologists could reach.

Our mission is to bridge the 4,500-year gap between the copper-tool era and the age of digital twin technology, ensuring that the legacy of Khufu remains accessible even as the physical stones face the slow erosion of the desert winds.
